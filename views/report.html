Narrative of What Accomplished Since Last Report:

· We worked on passing the above tests and have been able to pass all 43 test.

· We also made the site more ecstatically appealing by doing the following things:

1) add nice pictures and gifs;

2) beautify the website layout;

3) adjust word styles such as size and font；

4) polish the wording.

· We redesigned the app name, BestEventEver, and also the description of the app’s value proposition, which is to discover the best event in your life. The reason we made this change was because we asked others to read the former introduction of our app and they thought the value claim was indirect and unattractive to them. Our team didn’t have strong language skills, but we thought we could at least make the app name and value claim direct and simple, which is easy to be understood and remembered.

· We added more content to the website. For example, we completed the About Us page with our full information and pictures displaying.

· We also finished many challenges during the project process. The last and present sprint had a lot of back and forth movement of many items on the backlog as it took us time to get used to working on different branches and eventually merging and pushing them to the master branch without conflicts or totally losing the work done and having to do it all over again. The way we were trying to solve this problem was we helped remind each other to push our accomplished work into the master branch when we worked together.

· Team communication to make sure that at each point we were all on the same page was something we worked on and improved and which helped us hasten the pace of the work. The way we were trying to solve this problem was we set calendars on our outlook and sent emails to update each member’s working status once we were alarmed by our calendar.

· Another mistake we have made was that we didn’t push our work to Heroku each time we finished a sprint since we assumed we should push it at the end of the project. Then we had to push all our stories to Heroku at the end of our project.

· Another challenge we met was motivating each team member to finish the project. Since team members had different priorities during their academic year, someone laid back and someone was active in terms of accomplishing this project. Therefore, our team sort of lacked human resource since we had only 2 persons working on most of the project. We didn’t find a perfect solution for this problem. The way we were trying to overcome this challenge was to keep communicating with each other. Understand each other’s situation did help us make some progress.

· The last obstacle we had was persons in our team didn’t have a good sense of coding. We solved this problem by attending many TA sessions and Kyle’s office hours.
